Introduction

The clothes we wear are made from fabrics. Fabrics are made from fibres. In this chapter, we will learn about fibres, fabrics, and how they are made.

What Are Fibres?

Fibres are thin, thread-like structures used to make fabrics.

Sources of Fibres

Fibres are obtained from two main sources:

  • Natural fibres
  • Synthetic fibres

Natural Fibres üåø

Natural fibres are obtained from plants and animals.

Cotton Fibre üå±

Cotton is obtained from cotton plants. Cotton fibres are soft and comfortable.

Jute Fibre üåæ

Jute is obtained from jute plants. It is used to make ropes and bags.

Animal Fibres üêë

Wool is obtained from sheep, and silk is obtained from silkworms.

What Is Fabric?

Fabric is made by weaving or knitting fibres together.

  • Weaving ‚Äì Interlacing two sets of yarns

Summary

  • Fibres are used to make fabrics
  • Fibres can be natural or synthetic
  • Cotton and jute come from plants
  • Wool and silk come from animals
  • Fabric is made by weaving or knitting
