Introduction

Not all computers are the same. Some are small and portable, while others are large and powerful. In this lesson, we will learn about the different types of computers and how they are used in daily life.

1. Personal Computer (PC)

A Personal Computer is designed for use by one person at a time. It is commonly used at home, school, or office.

Examples:

  • Desktop computers used for work or gaming
  • Laptops used for study or travel

Uses:

  • Word processing, spreadsheets, and presentations
  • Online browsing, watching videos, or gaming

2. Laptop

A Laptop is a portable computer with a built-in screen, keyboard, and battery.

Examples:

  • Students use laptops for online classes
  • Professionals use laptops for office work

Uses:

  • Portable computing on the go
  • Internet browsing, document creation, and video calls

3. Tablet

A Tablet is a small, flat computer with a touchscreen.

Examples:

  • Reading e-books
  • Playing educational games
  • Browsing the internet

Uses:

  • Quick tasks and learning apps
  • Portable entertainment and communication

4. Server

A Server is a powerful computer that provides data and services to multiple computers over a network.

Examples:

  • Web servers hosting websites
  • School servers storing student records

Uses:

  • Managing multiple user requests
  • Storing and sharing data securely

5. Supercomputer

A Supercomputer is extremely fast and powerful, used for complex calculations and research.

Examples:

  • Weather forecasting
  • Scientific simulations
  • Space exploration

Uses:

  • High-speed computations for research
  • Handling large amounts of data quickly

Summary

  • Personal Computer is for individual use
  • Laptop is portable and battery-powered
  • Tablet is lightweight with a touchscreen
  • Server provides services to multiple users
  • Supercomputer is very powerful for special tasks

    • A. Laptop
